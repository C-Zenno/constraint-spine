# CG-FN-2 — Constraint Load and Bounded Persistence
**Foundation Note (Stage-0 / observer-only / non-authoritative)**

**Status:** v0.2
**Series:** Constraint Geometry (CG) — Foundation Notes

---

## Scope Notice

This Foundation Note defines vocabulary for constraint load, capacity, burden, and geometric response as governance primitives within the Constraint Geometry program.

It introduces no physical reinterpretations, no predictive claims, and no domain anchoring. It does not assert ontological identity between mass, energy, information, or computation.

This note remains Stage-0: refusal-first, vocabulary-bound, non-mechanistic.

---

## Purpose

- Define **constraint load** as a bound on persistence under admissible evolution.
- Define **capacity** and **burden** as claimability limits.
- Describe **geometric response** as relational adjustment under constraint.
- Prevent ontological conflations across domains.
- Establish vocabulary usable across physics, computation, governance, or other regimes without importing mechanism.

---

## 0.5 Non-Claims and Refusals (Stage-0)

This note does **not**:

- redefine physical theory,
- equate mass with information,
- equate energy with computation,
- treat geometry as generative mechanism,
- license predictive or collapse claims,
- introduce procedures, thresholds, or scoring systems.

Constraint load language defines admissibility limits only.

---

## 1. Constraint Load (Stage-0 Definition)

Constraint load names the bounded cost associated with maintaining or altering reassertable structure under admissible evolution.

**Constraint load is not substance.**
It is a descriptive term for how continuation regimes are limited.

Where greater constraint is required to preserve reassertability under declared bounds, constraint load is said to increase.

---

## 2. Capacity and Burden

### 2.1 Capacity

Capacity names the (declared) structural complexity ceiling that can remain reassertable under admissible evolution without violating claimability limits.

**Capacity is not intelligence, storage, or power.**
It is a bound on persistence.

### 2.2 Burden

Burden names the constraint load required to sustain a given continuation regime.

If burden exceeds capacity under declared bounds, continuation claims become inadmissible.

This defines a **claimability limit** — not a collapse prediction.

---

## 3. Geometric Response

Geometric response names relational adjustment under constraint load.

When persistence of structure is associated with bounded costs, relational configurations may adjust within admissible evolution.

**Geometry here is descriptive of relational structure, not a generative engine.**

No mechanism-level explanation is implied.

---

## 4. Constraint Load and Continuation

Constraint load bounds:

- which distinctions remain reassertable,
- which continuation regimes remain admissible,
- which identity claims can persist under evolution.

Increasing constraint load narrows admissible continuation classes.

This is a **contraction of claim-space**, not an ontological statement about reality.

---

## 5. Cross-Domain Neutrality

Constraint load vocabulary applies wherever:

- persistence has bounded cost,
- structure requires maintenance under evolution,
- continuation claims face capacity limits.

Physics may instantiate these patterns.
Computation may instantiate these patterns.
Governance systems may instantiate these patterns.

**This note anchors none of them.**

## 7. Receipts / Traceability

Stage-0 claims invoking constraint load, capacity, or burden must remain receiptable in principle:

- Declared bounds must be stated.
- View families must be attributable.
- Continuation regimes must be defined.
- Claimability limits must be explicit.

This note does not mandate a specific receipt schema.
It mandates that constraint load language cannot be used without declared admissibility context.

The program refuses:

- "mass is information"
- "geometry is computation"
- "constraint load proves ontology"
- "capacity implies consciousness"
- "burden predicts collapse"

**Constraint load defines admissibility limits only.**
