# CG-FN-4 - Transition and Carry
**Foundation Note (Stage-0 / observer-only / non-authoritative)**

**Status:** v0.1-draft
**Series:** Constraint Geometry (CG) - Foundation Notes
**Subtitle:** *Irreversibility, transport, and carried structure under bounded observation*

---

## Scope Notice

This Foundation Note defines transition and carry as claim-discipline vocabulary within the Constraint Geometry program.

It introduces no methods, thresholds, evaluators, protocols, routing rules, or operational transfer procedures. It does not define how systems move, synchronize, migrate, deploy, learn, or persist. It defines only the limits under which a claim that something has crossed a boundary may remain well-formed.

This note remains Stage-0: refusal-first, vocabulary-bound, non-mechanistic.

---

## Purpose

1. Define **transition** as a boundary-crossing claim, not an action primitive.
2. Define **carry** as bounded preservation of receiptable structure across transition.
3. Separate carry from identity, control, optimization, prediction, and authority.
4. Establish irreversibility as a receipt constraint, not a metaphysical claim.
5. Place transition and carry within the public claim discipline established in CG-FN-3.
6. Preserve the public-spine rule: receipts before reasons.

---

## 0.5 Non-Claims and Refusals (Stage-0)

This note is conceptual only. It does not define a transfer mechanism, deployment architecture, migration procedure, learning algorithm, endpoint, topology, runtime, or route. Transition and carry language defines claimability limits only; it does not claim that carried structure preserves identity, authority, capability, intent, interpretation, legitimacy, or continuation.

---

## 1. Opening Axiom

**Nothing carries by being named. It carries only by receipt under declared bounds.**

A transition claim asserts that a structure has crossed from one bounded context into another. A carry claim asserts that some structure remains receiptable after that crossing.

Neither claim is valid by narrative continuity alone.

Receipts before reasons.

---

## 2. Transition

Transition names a claim that a structure has crossed a boundary between bounded contexts.

The boundary may be temporal, representational, institutional, documentary, computational, or observational. The term does not privilege a substrate.

A transition claim must leave open refusal:

- the crossing may not have occurred,
- the crossed object may not be the claimed object,
- the carried structure may not be receiptable,
- the receiving context may change the admissible claim,
- the claim may exceed declared observation bounds.

Transition is therefore not movement-as-authority. It is a receiptable boundary-crossing claim.

---

## 3. Carry

Carry names the bounded preservation of a structure across transition.

Carry is not identity. It does not mean that the same system, person, object, authority, or capability persists. It means that a declared structure remains sufficiently receiptable, under declared bounds, for a limited continuation claim to be made.

A carry claim minimally requires:

- a source record,
- a receiving context,
- a declared boundary,
- a receiptable structure,
- a refusal condition,
- an exclusion statement.

Without these, "carry" becomes narrative transfer rather than claim discipline.

---

## 4. Irreversibility

Irreversibility names the fact that a boundary-crossing claim cannot be undone by later explanation.

A later record may amend, supersede, clarify, reject, or bound a transition claim. It cannot erase the fact that the earlier claim was made.

This is not a claim about physical time, metaphysics, or causality. It is a receipt constraint:

- records persist as records,
- later receipts do not delete prior receipt obligations,
- reasons do not precede admissible record,
- narrative repair does not convert an unreceipted carry claim into a receipted one.

Irreversibility is why transition requires bounded receipt at the point of claim.

---

## 5. Carry Is Not Continuation by Default

Continuation claims do not carry by default. They carry by permission under admissibility discipline.

Carry may support a continuation claim, but it does not guarantee one. A structure can be carried without preserving identity, authority, capability, intent, interpretation, or legitimacy.

The program therefore separates:

| Term | Claim |
|------|-------|
| Transition | A boundary crossing is being claimed |
| Carry | A bounded structure remains receiptable across the crossing |
| Continuation | A claim of persistence remains legitimate |
| Identity | A stronger claim that is not granted by carry alone |

Carry may be necessary for some continuation claims. It is not sufficient by itself.

---

## 6. Active Alignment, Passive Authority

Observer-only posture does not require late observation. It requires authority discipline.

An observer may maintain bounded temporal alignment with a structure so that, when the structure becomes receiptable, the observer is present enough to record it without claiming authority over it.

This is disciplined timeliness, not prediction.

The observer may be active in alignment. The observer remains passive in authority.

---

## 7. Transfer Without Laundering

Carry language must not launder authority from one context into another.

The following are refused:

- "it carried, therefore it is authorized",
- "it transitioned, therefore it continued",
- "it appears similar, therefore it is identical",
- "the receiving context accepted it, therefore the source claim was valid",
- "the record exists, therefore the claimed structure is true",
- "the structure arrived, therefore its reasons are established".

Receipt constrains the claim. It does not create excess authority.

## 9. Receipts / Traceability

Stage-0 transition and carry claims must remain receiptable in principle:

- source and receiving contexts must be distinguishable,
- the crossing boundary must be named at the level of claim,
- carried structure must be bounded,
- exclusions must be explicit,
- refusal must remain available,
- later continuation claims must not exceed the carry receipt.

This note does not mandate a receipt schema. It mandates that transition and carry language cannot be used without declared observation bounds.

---

## 10. Disallowed Conflations (Locked)

The program refuses:

- "carry equals identity",
- "transition equals continuation",
- "similarity equals persistence",
- "receipt equals proof",
- "arrival equals authority",
- "alignment equals prediction",
- "timeliness equals control",
- "irreversibility equals inevitability".

Transition and carry define claimability limits only.

It may name transition and carry boundaries; it must not be read as an implementation, evaluation, orchestration, enforcement, control, route, or authority-widening surface. Carried structure does not preserve identity or confer legitimacy by default.
