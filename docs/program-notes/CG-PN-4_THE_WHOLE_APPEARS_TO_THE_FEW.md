# CG-PN-4 -- The Whole Appears to the Few
**Program Note (Stage-0 / observer-only / non-authoritative)**
**Companion to:** CG-P-VIII -- *Appearance Under Bounded Resolution*
**Subtitle:** Constrained projection as a reading note for bounded appearance

---

## Scope Notice

This Program Note clarifies a reading boundary for **CG-P-VIII -- Appearance Under Bounded Resolution**.

It introduces no new paper identity, no procedures, no thresholds, no evaluators, and no operational criteria. It does not define a method for detecting wholes, reconstructing wholes, or ranking appearances.

This note is Stage-0: observer-only, refusal-first, non-authoritative, and doctrine-bound.

---

## Purpose

1. Preserve the distinction between **the whole** and **appearance of the whole**.
2. Define **constrained projection** as a reading aid for bounded observers.
3. Prevent bounded appearance from being upgraded into ontology, semantics, agency, or total knowledge.
4. Clarify that resolution-sensitive appearances may carry structure without carrying the whole.
5. Keep CG-P-VIII as the parent appearance artifact.

---

## 0.5 Non-Claims and Refusals (Stage-0)

This note does not claim:

- that the whole is directly observable,
- that a bounded appearance is equivalent to the whole,
- that projection reveals total structure,
- that appearance carries semantic authority,
- that resolution reconstructs ontology,
- that bounded observers can infer the whole from any single appearance.

A constrained projection may be receiptable as appearance.
It is not receiptable as the whole.

---

## 1. Parent Artifact

The parent artifact is:

**CG-P-VIII -- Appearance Under Bounded Resolution**

That paper owns the primary doctrine of:

- appearance under bounded view,
- contracted resolution,
- appearance classes,
- continuity versus appearance,
- and refusal against ontology, semantics, agency, or substrate claims.

This Program Note does not replace, rename, or duplicate CG-P-VIII.

It only clarifies one reading constraint:

> The whole may appear under bounded resolution, but it does not appear as the whole.

---

## 2. Constrained Projection

A **constrained projection** is a bounded appearance of a larger structure under limited resolution, window, or access.

The observer does not receive the whole.
The observer receives an appearance shaped by the constraints of the view.

A constrained projection may preserve some structure, relation, or surface regularity.
It does not preserve total availability.

In this sense:

> **The whole appears to the few, but not as the whole.**

---

## 3. The Few

"The few" names bounded observers, bounded windows, or bounded view positions.

It does not name a privileged class of agents, subjects, or interpreters.

The few are "few" because their access is finite:

- finite resolution,
- finite window,
- finite coupling,
- finite record,
- finite claimability.

The few may receive appearances of a larger structure.
They do not thereby receive the larger structure itself.

---

## 4. Appearance Is Not Total Access

Appearance is where something becomes available under a bounded view.

But availability is not identity.

A bounded appearance can be:

- structured,
- coherent,
- repeated,
- receiptable,
- and still not be the whole.

The fact that an appearance carries structure does not license the claim that it carries totality.

---

## 5. Resolution Sensitivity

Under bounded resolution, the same larger structure may appear differently across windows, scales, or access conditions.

This does not require contradiction.

Different appearances may be legitimate within different declared bounds.

A projection can be resolution-sensitive without being false.

A projection can be coherent without being complete.

A projection can be receiptable without being total.

---

## 6. Refusal Against Whole-Claim Inflation

This note refuses the upgrade from:

> appearance of the whole

to:

> possession of the whole.

It also refuses the upgrade from:

> structure preserved under projection

to:

> total structure reconstructed.

The proper Stage-0 claim is narrower:

> A bounded observer may receive a constrained projection whose appearance remains receiptable under declared bounds.

No stronger claim is licensed.

---

## 7. Relationship to Continuity

A constrained projection may appear continuous across a bounded window.

That does not establish continuity of the whole.

A continuity claim concerns the bounded relation among states or configurations across passage. It does not establish persistence of the whole.

A projected appearance may support a local continuity claim.
It does not automatically support a global one.

---

## 8. Relationship to Identity

A constrained projection may resemble the whole or resemble another projection.

Resemblance is not identity.

Identity requires supported persistence under declared bounds, not pointwise similarity or surface likeness.

This note therefore inherits the snapshot-to-identity refusal established in CG-FN-0.

---

## 9. Disallowed Conflations

The program refuses:

- appearance = whole,
- projection = reconstruction,
- resemblance = identity,
- local continuity = global continuity,
- structure = totality,
- coherence = completeness,
- resolution = ontology,
- bounded access = privileged access.

Constrained projection is a claim boundary, not a metaphysical bridge.

---
