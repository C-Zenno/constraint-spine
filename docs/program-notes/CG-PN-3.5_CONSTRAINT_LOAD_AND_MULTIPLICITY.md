# PN-AS-3.5 — Constraint Load and Multiplicity

### When continuation becomes a manifold

## Opening

**Systems do not continue by default.
They continue by permission.**

Continuation is therefore not an assumption about the world.
It is a **claim class** about whether a trajectory may be extended.

In earlier notes we established that continuation is binary:

* continuation permitted
* continuation refused

But the space in which continuation is evaluated is rarely static.
It moves as boundaries accumulate constraint load.

This produces a second condition that appears prior to refusal:

**multiplicity.**

---

# 1. Constraint load precedes structural change

In bounded systems, load signals appear before structural motion.

Constraint load may arise from:

* constraint mismatch
* resolution overload
* coordination load
* context divergence
* boundary rent escalation

Importantly:

**constraint load can be observable even when the boundary itself has not yet moved.**

A system may therefore detect that its current continuation regime is under strain before any visible expansion, fragmentation, or refusal occurs.

---

# 2. Admissible space is dynamic

Admissibility does not operate inside a fixed domain.

Instead the region of permissible continuation varies with the system's **continuity capacity**.

We may express this conceptually as a time-dependent region:

**A(t)**

where admissible continuation depends on the system's current ability to:

* observe
* retain structural continuity
* route interaction
* preserve authority boundaries
* maintain continuity of identity

Admissible space therefore expands or contracts with continuity capacity rather than with component count.

---

# 3. Multiplicity before settlement

When constraint load accumulates, continuation may remain permitted but no longer remain **single-valued** at the observer's resolution.

At this point the system encounters a manifold of admissible continuations.

This condition is not failure.

It is a **structural widening of the continuation class**.

Multiplicity may persist while:

* structural continuity remains intact
* boundary authority remains defined
* continuation remains admissible

Settlement occurs only when multiplicity can no longer remain structurally continuous or affordable.

---

# 4. Expansion does not guarantee capacity

Expansion of structure does not automatically enlarge admissible space.

Adding components without structural continuity may instead contract it.

Structural capacity therefore depends on relationships such as:

* structural continuity
* persistence
* routing clarity
* contextual alignment
* authority integrity
* boundary stability

Component count alone is not determinative.

---

# 5. Visibility and admissibility

Load signals, constraint receipts, and structural diagnostics correspond to increased evaluability of a system's own state.

Even when no structural mutation occurs, **reduced hidden uncertainty corresponds to a larger evaluable region.**

Where more of a system's constraint structure is observable, more of its continuation space is available for admissibility evaluation.

---

# 6. Disturbance projection

Failures observed within subsystems may represent projections of shared disturbances rather than independent causes.

A disturbance may propagate through constraint structure and appear as distinct subsystem errors.

Errors therefore represent **local constraint violations produced by the projection of shared disturbances across subsystems.**

Local anomalies may therefore be projections of shared disturbance rather than independent structural events.

---

# 7. Continuation and settlement

Multiplicity does not alter the fundamental rule.

A manifold of admissible trajectories may exist prior to settlement.

**The decision of continuation is binary: permitted or refused.**

When continuation is permitted, settlement selects one trajectory.

**The resulting claim remains singular.**

Only singular claims can be receipted.

---

# Closing

Constraint load reveals the boundaries of admissibility before those boundaries move.

Multiplicity appears when continuation remains permitted but no longer remains unique at the active resolution.

Settlement occurs when the manifold collapses into a trajectory that can be claimed.

**"Continue" is not inertia.
It is a governed act.**

---

# What this note does not claim

* No thresholds for constraint load or decision points
* No scoring or evaluation framework
* No operational branching logic
* No agent orchestration protocols
* No prescription for how multiplicity must collapse
* No claim that multiplicity violates physical determinism
* No control doctrine
