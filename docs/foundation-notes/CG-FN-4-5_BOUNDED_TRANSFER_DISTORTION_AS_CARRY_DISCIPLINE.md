# CG-FN-4.5 - Bounded Transfer Distortion as Carry Discipline
**Foundation Note (Stage-0 / observer-only / non-authoritative)**

**Status:** v0.1-draft
**Series:** Constraint Geometry (CG) - Foundation Notes
**Subtitle:** *Object-first transfer, receiving frames, and disciplined distortion claims*

---

## Scope Notice

This Foundation Note defines bounded transfer distortion as a claim-discipline concept within the Constraint Geometry program.

It introduces no transfer protocol, learning method, measurement procedure, benchmark, scoring rule, routing surface, or implementation architecture. It does not define how to move content, train systems, grade outputs, or validate models.

This note remains Stage-0: refusal-first, vocabulary-bound, non-mechanistic.

---

## Purpose

1. Define **bounded transfer distortion** as an expected claim hazard when structure crosses frames.
2. Separate distortion from error, failure, loss, and deception.
3. Preserve object-first discipline: the object constrains the transfer claim.
4. Prevent carry language from laundering source authority into receiving contexts.
5. State the minimum public refusal grammar for transfer claims.
6. Keep transfer doctrine non-operational and non-evaluative.

---

## 0.5 Non-Claims and Refusals (Stage-0)

This note is a public conceptual release. It defines bounded transfer distortion as a claimability hazard, not as an operational method.

This note does not define a transfer algorithm, model architecture, retrieval or memory method, benchmark, rubric, metric, leaderboard, deployment procedure, synchronization procedure, or procedure for converting private material into public release material.

It does not authorize model/API use, operational implementation, or disclosure of private mechanisms.

Bounded transfer distortion language defines claimability limits only. It does not claim that all distortion is harmful, or that transfer preserves identity, intent, source authority, or admissibility in the receiving frame.

---

## 1. Opening Axiom

**Every transfer crosses a receiving frame. Every receiving frame can distort what it carries.**

The question is not whether distortion exists. The question is whether the distortion is bounded, declared, and receiptable enough for the carry claim being made.

Receipts before reasons.

---

## 2. Bounded Transfer Distortion

Bounded transfer distortion names the difference between a source structure and the structure receiptable after it enters a receiving frame.

The receiving frame may alter:

- resolution,
- ordering,
- emphasis,
- omission,
- naming,
- dependency visibility,
- surface form,
- admissible claim ceiling.

Distortion is not automatically error. It becomes a claim hazard when it is hidden, unbounded, or used to justify more than the receipt permits.

---

## 3. Object-First Constraint

Object-first discipline means the object constrains the transfer claim.

The receiving frame does not get to decide, by convenience, what the transferred object was. It may receive, render, summarize, index, or stage a bounded structure, but the claim remains answerable to the source object and its declared exclusions.

Object-first transfer refuses:

- frame-first reinterpretation,
- audience-first simplification as authority,
- model-output convenience as evidence,
- summary as substitute object,
- resemblance as identity,
- ranking as understanding.

The object constrains what can be carried. The receiver constrains what can be observed. The receipt constrains what can be claimed.

---

## 4. Distortion Is Not Failure by Default

Some distortion is expected whenever a structure is carried into another frame.

A shortened index, visual card, abstract, mirror, routeback, digest, or public surface may legitimately expose less than the source object. That reduction is not failure when it is bounded and declared.

Failure begins when the reduced surface claims the authority of the whole object.

For Stage-0 purposes:

- bounded reduction may be admissible,
- undeclared reduction is risky,
- authority inflation is refused,
- hidden answer leakage is refused,
- source substitution is refused.

---

## 5. Carry Discipline Under Distortion

A transfer claim should preserve enough structure to make refusal possible.

At minimum, the claim should be able to say:

- what source object is being carried,
- what receiving frame is involved,
- what is intentionally omitted,
- what cannot be claimed from the transfer,
- what receipt anchors the transfer,
- what would count as overclaim.

This is not a checklist procedure. It is a refusal grammar for public claim discipline.

---

## 6. Relation to Transition and Carry

FN-4 defines transition and carry.

FN-4.5 names the main hazard inside carry: the receiving frame can change what becomes visible, emphasized, omitted, or claimable.

Therefore, "carried" does not mean "unchanged." It means that a bounded structure remains receiptable despite the receiving frame.

If the receiving frame changes the structure beyond declared bounds, the carry claim must narrow or fail.

---

## 7. Public and Private Surfaces

Public transfer surfaces must not imply that unpublished, private, held-out, or operator-side structure has been fully exposed.

A public card, digest, landing page, paper index, or spine entry can point to an object without carrying all of it.

The public surface must not:

- expose private answer keys,
- imply private governance authority,
- collapse study artifacts into public claims,
- convert an internal object into public doctrine by summary alone,
- erase provenance or receipt obligations.

The public surface is a carried view. It is not the source object unless explicitly designated as such.

---

## 8. Transfer and Learning Claims

When transfer language is used near learning, memory, retrieval, or evaluation, the claim ceiling narrows.

Stage-0 permits vocabulary about:

- source object,
- receiving frame,
- carried view,
- omitted structure,
- distortion risk,
- refusal boundary.

Stage-0 does not permit:

- capability claims,
- model-ranking claims,
- benchmark claims,
- training claims,
- inference claims,
- substrate superiority claims,
- claims that a system "understood" an object because it reproduced a surface.

Learning-adjacent transfer remains object-first and receipt-bound.

---

## 9. Receipts / Traceability

Stage-0 transfer claims must remain receiptable in principle:

- the source object must be identifiable,
- the carried view must be bounded,
- the receiving frame must be distinguishable from the source,
- omissions and exclusions must not be hidden,
- authority must not widen during transfer,
- later claims must not exceed the transfer receipt.

This note does not mandate a receipt schema. It mandates that transfer language cannot be used to bypass declared observation bounds.

---

## 10. Disallowed Conflations (Locked)

The program refuses:

- "transfer equals preservation",
- "summary equals source",
- "mirror equals authority",
- "view equals whole object",
- "resemblance equals identity",
- "model output equals understanding",
- "public card equals public release of all structure",
- "bounded distortion equals defect by default",
- "receipted transfer equals capability claim".

Bounded transfer distortion defines claimability limits only.
