# CG-PN-4.5 -- Bounded Transfer Distortion
**Program Note (Stage-0 / observer-only / non-authoritative)**
**Sub-numbered extension of:** CG-PN-4 -- *The Whole Appears to the Few*
**Companion to:** CG-P-0-B -- *Object-First Learning Under Bounded Frames*
**Subtitle:** Boundary-crossing claim hazards as an intermediate vocabulary under Carry

---

## Scope Notice

This Program Note records vocabulary for **bounded transfer distortion**, the parent term introduced in Section 11 of CG-P-0-B v1.1.

The catalog identifier **CG-PN-4.5** is a deliberate sub-numbering. It positions this note as a scoped extension of CG-PN-4 and mirrors framework Section 4.5 under Carry. The artifact is a hinge in both the canon and the framework, not a new pillar or method.

It introduces no new paper identity, no procedures, no thresholds, no evaluators, no operational criteria, and no detection methods. It does not define a method for measuring transfer, ranking receivers, or remediating distortion.

This note remains Stage-0: observer-only, refusal-first, non-authoritative, and doctrine-bound. Its **Published** status settles this vocabulary and refusal posture. It does not raise the claim ceiling or authorize application.

---

## Purpose

1. Preserve the distinction between a claimed boundary crossing and a carry claim supported by receiptable structure under declared bounds.
2. Name **bounded transfer distortion** as the difference between source structure and structure receiptable in a receiving frame, and as a claim hazard when that difference is hidden, unbounded, or used to support more than the declared bounds permit.
3. Position this vocabulary as **Section 4.5 under Carry**, between the Carry pillar and adjacent learning-side material.
4. Sub-number into CG-PN-4's canon node as **CG-PN-4.5**, signalling that this note is a tight extension of CG-PN-4 rather than a successor.
5. Prevent bounded appearance from being upgraded into transferable totality.
6. Refuse the upgrade from recurrence or apparent movement to an admissible carry claim.
7. Keep CG-P-0-B as the parent learning-side artifact and CG-PN-4 as the upstream refusal anchor.

## 3. Definition

**Bounded transfer distortion** names the difference between a source structure and the structure receiptable in a receiving frame.

That difference becomes a claim hazard when it is hidden, unbounded, or used to support more than the declared bounds permit.

Pattern recurrence or apparent movement establishes, at most, a transition claim. Carry requires bounded support that declared structure remained receiptable across the crossing. When receiving-frame conditions cannot support the original claim, the admissible result is claim narrowing or refusal. Distortion is not failure by default; bounded, declared reduction may remain admissible.

The central issue is whether a bounded carried view preserves its partiality rather than being asserted as the whole.

> It is whether the carried view remains receiptable without mistaking the few for the whole.

This axis is inherited directly from CG-PN-4's refusal against whole-claim inflation.

---

## 4. Axes

These modes do not share one axis. They name distinct dimensions along which receiving-frame difference can alter the admissibility of a carry claim. The taxonomy is **axis-aware**, not flat.

1. **Boundary direction** -- the declared source and receiving frames of the carry claim.
2. **Scope error** -- whether the claim is applied too narrowly, too broadly, or at the wrong level.
3. **Frame integrity** -- whether the source conditions remain supportable in the receiving frame.
4. **Outcome severity** -- the consequence for the claim, ranging from within-bounds distortion through claim narrowing to refusal.
5. **Whole-from-few visibility** -- whether the receiving-frame claim preserves the partiality of the carried view rather than inflating it into the whole.

The fifth axis is the deepest. It is the direct downstream of CG-PN-4. A carried view is always bounded relative to its source object, and any admissible claim remains bounded by that partiality.

These axes are descriptive, not measurable. No metric is defined on any axis.

---

## 5. Subtypes

The following are **root subtypes** of bounded transfer distortion. Each names a distinct claim shape that may be located along the axes above; no subtype is a sum of the others.

**Under-carried transfer**
The carried view supports less of the declared source structure than the carry claim asserts. A narrower claim may remain admissible.

**Mis-carried transfer**
The carried view is attributed to a receiving frame in which the source relations are not supportable under declared bounds.

**Over-carried transfer**
The carry claim extends the structure beyond declared source or receiving-frame bounds.

**Frozen transfer**
The source framing is repeated without declaring changed receiving-frame conditions. Surface recurrence is not evidence that the original bounds still hold.

**Maladaptive transfer**
The severe claim shape: a carried view supports a local claim while constraining or defeating later carry claims. This subtype is **demoted from a standalone case to a bounded subtype** within the parent class; it does not stand as the umbrella category and does not carry an evaluative claim about the receiver.

---

## 6. On "Over-applied Transfer"

Over-applied transfer is sometimes named as a peer category. In this note it is treated as an **expression mode**, not a root subtype.

Over-application may coincide with:

- over-carried transfer,
- mis-carried transfer,
- frozen transfer,
- scope widening,
- unsupported analogy,
- undeclared receiving-frame change.

It is a surface description that may appear with one or more root subtypes. It is not a cause, procedure, or root subtype.

---

## 7. The Few and the Whole (Inheritance from CG-PN-4)

CG-PN-4 names "the few" as bounded observers, bounded windows, or bounded view positions, whose access is finite in resolution, window, coupling, record, and claimability.

CG-PN-4.5 inherits that vocabulary and turns it toward Carry.

The few may receive bounded appearances of a larger structure. They do not thereby receive the larger structure itself. A claim that such a view was carried remains separately bounded. **Bounded transfer distortion is the claim hazard in which a bounded carried view is asserted as though it were the whole.**

A bounded appearance can be:

- structured,
- coherent,
- repeated,
- receiptable,
- and still not be the whole.

A coherent appearance crossing a boundary does not, by itself, establish receiptable carry or license a claim to the whole.

---

## 8. Disallowed Conflations

This note refuses:

- transfer = learning,
- carry = totality,
- pattern appears in a receiving frame = carry claim is admissible,
- bounded appearance = fully carried structure,
- local navigation = structural understanding,
- repeated surface = preserved source conditions,
- coherent carried view = admissible carry claim,
- partial exposure = whole-object support,
- few = whole,
- absence of observed error = preservation of declared bounds.

Bounded transfer distortion is a vocabulary boundary, not a diagnostic instrument.

---

## 9. Refusal Against Carry-as-Totality

This note refuses the upgrade from:

> a pattern appears in a receiving frame

to:

> the carry claim is admissible under the source bounds.

It also refuses the upgrade from:

> the receiving surface reproduces the pattern

to:

> the source structure was preserved in full.

The proper Stage-0 claim is narrower:

> A bounded carried view may appear in a receiving frame. Whether declared structure remains receiptable under the source and receiving bounds is a separate claim that recurrence or apparent movement does not settle.

No stronger claim is licensed.
