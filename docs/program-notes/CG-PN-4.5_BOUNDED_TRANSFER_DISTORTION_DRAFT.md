# CG-PN-4.5 -- Bounded Transfer Distortion (Draft)
**Program Note (Stage-0 / observer-only / non-authoritative / Draft)**
**Sub-numbered extension of:** CG-PN-4 -- *The Whole Appears to the Few*
**Companion to:** CG-P-0-B -- *Object-First Learning Under Bounded Frames*
**Subtitle:** Boundary-crossing diagnostics as an intermediate vocabulary under Carry

---

## Scope Notice

This Program Note records vocabulary for **bounded transfer distortion**, the parent term introduced as a §11 subsection in CG-P-0-B v1.1.

The catalog identifier **CG-PN-4.5** is a deliberate sub-numbering. It positions this note between CG-PN-4 and the future CG-PN-5 just as the framework section §4.5 sits between §4 Carry and §5 the later learning framework. The artifact is a hinge in both the canon and the framework.

It introduces no new paper identity, no procedures, no thresholds, no evaluators, no operational criteria, and no detection methods. It does not define a method for measuring transfer, ranking learners, or remediating distortion.

This note is Stage-0: observer-only, refusal-first, non-authoritative, and doctrine-bound. It is published in **Draft** posture and may be revised as the vocabulary is exercised against further artifacts.

---

## Purpose

1. Preserve the distinction between **transfer** and **valid transfer**.
2. Name **bounded transfer distortion** as the failure mode in which carried structure moves across a boundary without preserving the bounds that made it valid.
3. Position this vocabulary as **§4.5 under Carry**, between the Carry pillar and the later learning/transfer framework.
4. Sub-number into CG-PN-4's canon node as **CG-PN-4.5**, signalling that this note is a tight extension of CG-PN-4 rather than a successor.
5. Prevent bounded appearance from being upgraded into transferable totality.
6. Refuse the upgrade from "pattern crossed a boundary" to "pattern remains valid across the boundary."
7. Keep CG-P-0-B as the parent learning-side artifact and CG-PN-4 as the upstream refusal anchor.

- that all transfer is suspect,

- that learners can be classified by which subtype they exhibit,

## 3. Definition

**Bounded transfer distortion** names the failure mode in which carried structure moves across a boundary without preserving the bounds that made it valid in its origin frame.

Carry is not automatically learning. A pattern can move across frames while losing the boundary conditions that made it valid. Bounded transfer distortion names the case where transfer occurs, but the transferred structure no longer preserves the frame required for correct use.

The central axis is not merely whether the learner can transfer from one case to another.

> It is whether the learner can see enough of the whole from the few without mistaking the few for the whole.

This axis is inherited directly from CG-PN-4's refusal against whole-claim inflation.

---

## 4. Axes

These modes do not share one axis. They name distinct dimensions along which transfer may fail to preserve frame integrity. The taxonomy is **axis-aware**, not flat.

1. **Boundary direction** -- where the pattern is being carried from and to.
2. **Scope error** -- whether the pattern is applied too narrowly, too broadly, or at the wrong level.
3. **Frame integrity** -- whether the conditions that stabilized the pattern in its origin frame survive the crossing.
4. **Outcome severity** -- ranging from harmless mismatch through blocking distortion to maladaptive distortion.
5. **Whole-from-few visibility** -- whether the learner can infer the larger pattern from partial exposure without overclaiming the whole from the few.

The fifth axis is the deepest. It is the direct downstream of CG-PN-4. A learner's exposure is always partial, and the question is whether what they carry forward respects that partiality.

These axes are descriptive, not measurable. No metric is defined on any axis.

---

## 5. Subtypes

The following are **root subtypes** of bounded transfer distortion. Each names a distinct failure shape that may be located along the axes above; no subtype is a sum of the others.

**Under-carried transfer**
A useful pattern fails to cross the boundary. Structure remains trapped in its origin frame and does not become available where it could have remained admissible.

**Mis-carried transfer**
A pattern crosses the boundary into the wrong frame. The carried structure may be intact, but it lands somewhere its relations no longer hold.

**Over-carried transfer**
A pattern crosses too far. The structure is extended into frames beyond its admissibility.

**Frozen transfer**
A pattern repeats across frames without reframing. The structure travels but cannot adapt to the new frame's conditions.

**Maladaptive transfer**
The severe case: a carried structure preserves local navigation while actively blocking or weakening future transfer. This subtype is **demoted from a standalone case to a bounded subtype** within the parent class; it does not stand as the umbrella category and does not carry an evaluative claim about the learner.

---

## 6. On "Over-applied Transfer"

Over-applied transfer is sometimes named as a peer category. In this note it is treated as an **expression mode**, not a root subtype.

Over-application can result from:

- over-carried transfer,
- mis-carried transfer,
- frozen transfer,
- premature abstraction,
- false analogy,
- weak boundary detection.

It is a *summation behavior* visible at the surface of one or more of the root subtypes. It belongs in the explanation of how the root subtypes appear in practice, not in the root list.

---

## 7. The Few and the Whole (Inheritance from CG-PN-4)

CG-PN-4 names "the few" as bounded observers, bounded windows, or bounded view positions, whose access is finite in resolution, window, coupling, record, and claimability.

CG-PN-4.5 inherits that vocabulary and turns it toward Carry.

The few may receive appearances of a larger structure. They do not thereby receive the larger structure itself. **Bounded transfer distortion is the failure mode in which the few carry what they have seen as if it were the whole.**

A bounded appearance can be:

- structured,
- coherent,
- repeated,
- receiptable,
- and still not be the whole.

Carrying a coherent appearance across a boundary does not license the claim that the whole has been carried.

---

## 8. Disallowed Conflations

This note refuses:

- transfer = learning,
- carry = totality,
- pattern crossed a boundary = pattern remains valid across the boundary,
- bounded appearance = transferable structure,
- local navigation = structural understanding,
- repeated pattern = adapted pattern,
- coherent transfer = correct transfer,
- partial exposure = sufficient sample,
- few = whole,
- absence of error = preservation of bounds.

Bounded transfer distortion is a vocabulary boundary, not a diagnostic instrument.

---

## 9. Refusal Against Carry-as-Totality

This note refuses the upgrade from:

> a pattern has been carried across a boundary

to:

> the pattern remains valid in its new frame.

It also refuses the upgrade from:

> the learner repeats the pattern in a new setting

to:

> the learner has transferred the structure.

The proper Stage-0 claim is narrower:

> A bounded observer may carry a pattern across a boundary; whether the carried pattern remains admissible in the new frame is a separate question that no movement of the pattern, by itself, settles.

No stronger claim is licensed.
