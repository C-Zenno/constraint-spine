# CG-FN-5 - Identity as Bounded Sameness Claim

## Catalog Description

**FN-5 - Identity as Bounded Sameness Claim**

Defines identity as the admissible sameness-claim that remains receiptable under bounded passage. Separates continuity from identity, holder from custodian, projection from transfer, record from total identity, and distortion from collapse.

---

## Object

Identity is not continuity itself.

Continuity may relate states across passage without establishing persistence. Identity is the admissible sameness-claim that may be supported over receipted persistence after bounded passage.

An identity claim does not settle what the object ultimately is. It concerns what bounded distinction may still legitimately be called the same after passage.

## Core Lock

Identity is not persistence alone.

Identity is a bounded sameness-claim over supported persistence.

The word "same" is admissible only inside declared receipt bounds.

Passage does not preserve the whole identity-system by default. Passage preserves only what the receipt envelope can still support.

---

## Sameness Claim Discipline

The word "same" is admissible only inside declared bounds.

Same object does not mean same appearance.
Same holder does not mean same custodian.
Same record does not mean complete memory.
Same projection does not mean transfer.
Same role does not mean same identity.
Same identity does not mean unchanged surface.

A sameness claim is bounded by what remains same, under what passage envelope, and what has been demoted, compressed, hidden, transferred, projected, or refused.

---

## Holder / Custodian Distinction

Holder names the bounded object to which the identity claim is attributed.

Custodian names a temporary or bounded carrier of the surface, record, role, artifact, custody path, or projection through which identity evidence may pass.

A custodian may carry identity evidence without becoming the holder.

Holder-substitution occurs when a custodian, witness, role, audience, account, institution, model, file, or public surface is treated as the unitary holder of the identity-system.

---

## Core Axioms

**A1. Boundary Requirement**
Identity requires a bounded distinction.

**A2. Receiptable Sameness**
Identity persists only where a bounded sameness-claim remains receiptable across passage.

**A3. Passage Non-Totality**
Passage may preserve, distort, compress, demote, or refuse an identity claim. It does not preserve the whole identity-system by default.

**A4. Projection Non-Transfer**
Projection is not transfer. An identity may appear through another surface without becoming that surface.

**A5. Record Limitation**
Record supports identity claims but does not exhaust identity.

**A6. Holder Non-Substitution**
Holder attribution is not substituted by witness, role, custodian, surface, audience, file, institution, model, or projection.

**A7. Refusal Closure**
When the receipt envelope no longer supports the sameness claim at the asserted strength, the admissible stance is demotion, non-settlement, or refusal.

**A8. Continuity Distinction**
Identity is not continuity. Continuity relates states across passage; identity is the bounded sameness-claim that may be supported over persistence after passage.

## Identity Receipt Envelope

An identity claim is admissible only where the following remain declared or bounded:

1. object
2. origin
3. holder
4. boundary
5. record
6. custody path
7. role limits
8. dependency conditions
9. projection limits
10. passage event
11. view / claim bounds
12. refusal condition
13. settlement state

Short form:

object / origin / holder / boundary / record / custody / projection / passage / claim bounds / refusal / settlement

---

## Settlement States

**PERSISTED**
The same bounded distinction remains receiptable under the declared passage envelope.

**DEMOTED**
A weaker identity claim remains receiptable, while the stronger sameness claim is no longer admissible at that strength.

**UNSETTLED**
The receipt envelope is incomplete. Collapse is not claimed, but sameness is not yet admissible.

**REFUSED**
No admissible identity claim can be made under current bounds.

Collapse is reached only when no bounded sameness-claim remains receiptable under declared bounds.

## Passage Rule

Passage does not preserve the whole identity-system by default.

Passage preserves only what the receipt envelope can still support.

If appearance changes, identity may persist.
If role changes, identity may persist.
If custody changes, identity may persist.
If record compresses, identity may persist.
If projection changes, identity may persist.

If the sameness claim outruns the receipt envelope, the claim demotes, remains unsettled, or is refused.
