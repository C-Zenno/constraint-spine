# DEF_CONTINUITY — Continuity

## Definition

The bounded relation among states or configurations across passage under which admissible ordering remains composable.

Continuity is a claim condition on composability. It does not by itself establish persistence. It is not a smoothness assumption, not a mechanism, and not an operational test.

## Non-equivalences

- Continuity is not smoothness (in the analytic sense).
- Continuity is not stability (stability implies perturbation response; continuity states a bounded relation across passage).
- Continuity is not persistence (persistence requires a declared distinction to remain supportable and reassertable after passage).
- Continuity is not identity (identity is a bounded sameness-claim over supported persistence).
- Continuity is not memory (memory concerns what remains carryable from past content; continuity concerns relational composability across passage).

## Exclusions

- No detection procedure for continuity is defined or implied.
- No threshold for "sufficient continuity" is stated.
- No metric or measure of continuity is provided.
- No operational test for continuity failure is given.
