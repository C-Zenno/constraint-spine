# CG-FN-3 — Admissibility as Claim Discipline
**Foundation Note (Stage-0 / observer-only / non-authoritative)**

**Status:** v0.2
**Series:** Constraint Geometry (CG) — Foundation Notes
**Subtitle:** *Continuation, settlement, and refusal under bounded observation*

---

## Scope Notice

This Foundation Note defines the scope and exclusions of Admissibility Studies as a discipline of claim legitimacy within the Constraint Geometry program.

It introduces no methods, thresholds, evaluators, or operational criteria. It does not define how systems should behave. It defines when claims about their continuation remain legitimate.

This note remains Stage-0: refusal-first, vocabulary-bound, non-mechanistic.

---

## Purpose

1. Define Admissibility Studies as a discipline of claim legitimacy, not control.
2. Establish the minimal grammar: Record → Receipt → Refusal → Continuation.
3. Define continuation as binary at the level of claim discipline.
4. Define multiplicity as pre-settlement admissible geometry.
5. Define settlement as claim fixation from within admissible multiplicity.
6. Establish refusal as constitutive of admissibility, not ancillary to it.
7. Separate admissibility from ontology, implementation, and governance policy.

---

## 0.5 Non-Claims and Refusals (Stage-0)

This note does not:

- introduce methods, thresholds, evaluators, or scoring,
- define an authorization mechanism or decision procedure,
- define a policy framework or enforcement doctrine,
- specify runtime orchestration or control,
- claim that admissibility causes continuation,
- license optimization, prediction, or recommendation,
- define what exists (ontology) or how systems are built (implementation).

Admissibility Studies defines claimability limits only.

---

## 1. Opening Axiom

**Continuation claims do not carry by default. They carry by permission.**

Here, "permission" names the condition under which a continuation claim may remain legitimately carried under bounded observation. It does not name command, control, enforcement, or runtime authorization.

This note concerns the discipline under which such permission may be claimed.

Admissibility Studies is not a theory of control. It is not a policy framework. It is not a runtime specification.

It is a discipline of claim legitimacy.

---

## 2. What Admissibility Studies Is

Admissibility Studies examines the conditions under which a claim of continuation may remain well-formed under bounded observation.

Its objects are not systems as such, but the claims made about systems.

It asks:

- what may be asserted,
- what must precede assertion,
- what must remain receiptable,
- and when continuation must be refused.

Admissibility therefore governs claimability, not ontology and not implementation.

---

## 3. The Minimal Grammar

Admissibility Studies proceeds through a minimal sequence:

**Record → Receipt → Refusal → Continuation**

This sequence names a **dependency structure for claim legitimacy**, not a temporal procedure or execution flow.

**Record** — A bounded trace attached to a claim.

**Receipt** — A carryable record sufficient to constrain what may be claimed.

**Refusal** — A permitted structural outcome in which continuation is not carried.

**Continuation** — The binary allowance for a claim to remain within a declared regime.

This is a grammar of observer legitimacy, not a mechanism of world-behavior.

---

## 4. Continuation Is Binary

Continuation, at the level of claim discipline, is binary: **carried or refused**.

Gradation belongs to record quality, observation limits, or regime description — not to continuation itself.

---

## 5. Multiplicity Does Not Alter the Rule

A manifold of admissible trajectories may exist prior to settlement.

This condition is called **multiplicity**.

Multiplicity does not alter the binary nature of continuation. It describes the geometry of admissible possibility before a claim is fixed.

A continuation may therefore remain permitted while multiple admissible trajectories still exist.

---

## 6. Settlement Yields Singular Claim

**Settlement** names the fixation of a receiptable claim from within a previously admissible multiplicity.

Multiplicity may exist before settlement. The claim does not.

The decision of continuation is binary. The resulting claim is singular.

Only singular claims can be receipted.

This distinction is foundational:

- multiplicity concerns admissible space,
- continuation concerns permission,
- settlement concerns claim fixation.

These must not be collapsed.

---

## 7. Record Precedes Legitimacy

Legitimacy does not begin with explanation. It begins with record.

A record establishes what can be carried without reinterpretation. A receipt constrains what can be claimed from that record.

Without record, legitimacy drifts into narrative. Without receipt, narrative hardens into authority.

Admissibility therefore begins from bounded trace, not persuasion.

---

## 8. Refusal Preserves the Boundary

Refusal is not a failure of the system. It is the **function of the boundary**.

Without refusal, continuation becomes automatic. When continuation becomes automatic, admissibility disappears.

Refusal preserves the distinction between:

- what may be carried,
- what may be observed,
- and what must stop.

For this reason, refusal is not ancillary to admissibility. It is constitutive of it.

---

## 9. Ontology and Implementation Remain Separate

Admissibility Studies does not define what exists. That belongs to ontology.

It does not define how systems are built. That belongs to architecture and runtime.

Its proper object is narrower:

**the legitimacy of continuation claims under bounded observation.**

This note therefore sits between ontology and implementation.

It receives structure from upstream theory. It refuses operationalization downstream.

---

## 10. Boundary of the Lane

Admissibility Studies is the lane in which:

- claims are disciplined,
- continuations are bounded,
- settlements are fixed,
- and refusals remain possible.

It is upstream of runtime. It is downstream of ontology. It is adjacent to governance, but not reducible to policy language.

Its task is not to manage systems.

Its task is to keep claims from exceeding what their receipts can carry.

## 12. Receipts / Traceability

Stage-0 claims invoking admissibility, continuation, settlement, or refusal must remain receiptable in principle:

- Records must be bounded and traceable.
- Receipts must constrain what may be claimed.
- Refusals must be structurally permitted outcomes.
- Continuation claims must be binary and explicit.
- Settlement must yield singular, receiptable claims.

This note does not mandate a specific receipt schema. It mandates that admissibility language cannot be used without declared observation bounds.
