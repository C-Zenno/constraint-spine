# CG-FN-6 - Persistence Before Identity
**Foundation Note (Stage-0 / observer-only / non-authoritative)**

**Status:** v0.1-draft

**Series:** Constraint Geometry (CG) - Foundation Notes

**Subtitle:** *Claim dependency across continuation, continuity, persistence, and identity*

---

## Scope Notice

This Foundation Note consolidates the corrected claim dependency among continuation, continuity, persistence, evidence supporting persistence, causal inheritance, and identity claims across bounded passage.

It records a disclosed terminology alignment across the Foundation Notes, Program Notes, glossary, hierarchy, and public continuity definition. Earlier tagged versions remain part of the historical record.

"Before" names logical support within claim discipline. It does not name chronology, ontology, workflow, or execution order.

This note remains Stage-0: refusal-first, vocabulary-bound, non-mechanistic, and non-predictive.

---

## Purpose

* Distinguish continuation, continuity, persistence, evidence supporting persistence, and identity.
* State persistence as upstream of identity within claim dependency.
* Record the aligned meanings without hiding the revision from earlier versions.
* Preserve the distinction between persistence and its supporting evidence.
* Define causal inheritance without equating inheritance with sameness.
* Clarify that persistence may exceed any single bounded identity claim.
* Prevent persistence from being upgraded into claims about life, consciousness, personhood, or purpose.

---

## 0.5 Non-Claims and Refusals (Stage-0)

This note does not:

* treat persistence as a substance or universal state,
* erase or conceal the displaced wording preserved in earlier tagged versions,
* equate persistence with identity,
* equate causal inheritance with sameness,
* infer life, consciousness, personhood, or purpose from persistence,
* provide methods, evaluators, thresholds, or operational rules.

This note defines vocabulary, claim dependency, and refusal boundaries only.

---

## 1. Object

Persistence is not identity.

Persistence names what remains supportable after passage under declared bounds.

Identity names the bounded sameness-claim that may become admissible over supported persistence under those bounds.

> **Persistence names what remained supportable after passage. Identity names what may still be called the same.**

---

## 2. Claim Dependency

This note consolidates the aligned meanings now recorded across the canon. It does not arrange them as operational stages.

An identity claim depends on bounded support for persistence beyond snapshot resemblance.

Neither continuation nor continuity alone settles an identity claim.

Persistence alone does not settle an identity claim.

The dependency asserted here is narrower:

> **Persistence is upstream of identity as a claim dependency.**

"Upstream" names logical support within claim discipline. It does not name chronology, ontology, workflow, or execution order.

Continuation does not establish continuity.

Continuity does not establish persistence.

Persistence does not establish identity.

Identity does not retroactively produce what persisted.

---

## 3. Core Definitions

### 3.1 Continuation

**Continuation** concerns whether sufficient declared structure remains available for carry through bounded passage.

At the level of claim discipline, a continuation claim is permitted or refused. Continuation does not establish that carry occurred.

### 3.2 Continuity

**Continuity** is the bounded relation among states or configurations across passage.

Continuity does not itself establish that a declared distinction remained supportable afterward.

### 3.3 Persistence

**Persistence** names a declared distinction that remains supportable and reassertable after passage under stated bounds.

Persistence does not by itself establish sameness.

### 3.4 Persistence Receipt

A **persistence receipt** is bounded evidence supporting a persistence claim.

The receipt is not the persistence itself.

> **Persistence is not its receipt.**

> **The object of a claim is not the evidence supporting it.**

### 3.5 Identity

**Identity** is the bounded sameness-claim that may become admissible over supported persistence under declared bounds.

For this note, identity depends on support for persistence under declared bounds, but persistence does not compel identity.

---

## 4. Locked First Proposition

> **P1. Evidenced persistence may precede and exceed the admission of any single bounded identity claim.**

"Precede" names admissibility order, not chronology or ontology.

"Exceed" means that the supported persistence distinction may be broader than any single bounded sameness-claim warranted over it.

An identity claim may change or end without establishing that nothing persisted.

---

## 5. Persistence Across Identity-Claim Boundaries

A change in description, appearance, role, custody, record, or projection may narrow or end one bounded identity claim while a persistence claim remains supportable.

This does not preserve the whole prior identity. It supports only the persistence claim warranted by the available evidence.

> **An identity claim may change or end while a narrower persistence claim remains supportable.**

---

## 6. Causal Inheritance

**Causal inheritance** names the bounded claim that an earlier condition remained consequential after passage.

Causal inheritance may remain supportable when an identity claim changes or ends.

Causal inheritance does not establish sameness or identity.

A persistence claim concerns what remained supportable after passage. An identity claim concerns what bounded distinction may still legitimately be called the same. These claims remain distinct.

---

## 7. Claim Strength and Refusal

When evidence no longer supports a persistence claim at its asserted strength, a narrower claim may remain supportable.

Refusal of an identity claim does not automatically refuse every persistence claim.

Refusal applies only to the claim under review and does not establish universal absence.

---

## 9. Receipts / Traceability

A persistence claim remains admissible only under a declared object, stated bounds, and supporting evidence.

The evidence supports only the persistence distinction warranted under those bounds. It does not establish total identity or persistence beyond the declared bounds.

This note defines bounded evidentiary discipline only. Operational forms remain outside its scope.

---

## 10. Disallowed Conflations (Locked)

The program refuses:

* "persistence is identity"
* "continuity is identity"
* "causal inheritance proves sameness"
* "the receipt is the persistence"
* "snapshot resemblance proves persistence"
* "record, material, role, or custody continuity proves total identity"
* "persistence proves life, consciousness, personhood, or purpose"
* "refusal proves nonexistence"

Persistence supports only the claim warranted under declared bounds.
